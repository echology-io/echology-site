Echology is the perception layer for structured AI. We classify and structure knowledge before it reaches a model. Applications built on the platform apply this architecture to specific industries.

The Problem

Documents contain structure. Most AI ignores it.

Every firm drowns in documents. Specs, contracts, reports, submittals, change orders. Thousands per project. Millions per firm.

Existing AI tools treat documents as flat text. They dump content into a context window and hope for the best. But documents have structure: authority levels, risk classifications, legal obligations, cross-references, numerical precision requirements. Flat text loses all of that.

Echology builds the missing layer. Our platform decomposes documents into classified semantic units, verifies claims against known standards, and produces auditable intelligence. Deterministic classification before probabilistic generation.

Principles

Local-first

Your data stays on your hardware. No cloud. No API keys. No data leaving the building. This isn't a feature. It's a requirement for firms that handle privileged, ITAR-controlled, or client-confidential content.

Perception before generation

The system sees before it speaks. Documents are decomposed into classified semantic units before any generative model is invoked. Deterministic classification identifies what matters. Every decision is logged, explainable, and auditable.

Deterministic where it counts

AI is powerful but unpredictable. We use AI for reasoning, but wrap it in deterministic systems: hash-chained audit trails, regex-based authority detection, schema validation. The output is reproducible and defensible.

Built for professionals

Our users are engineers, PMs, and principals at firms that build bridges, hospitals, and power plants. They need tools that are accurate, accountable, and auditable. Not chatbots. Intelligence infrastructure.

Company

Kyle spent 13+ years in geotechnical and civil engineering. He acquired and ran a geotech firm. His last role was at LaBella Associates (ENR Top 200), where he adopted AI tools and validated them across a team of 200 engineers. The tools stuck. That experience, deploying AI inside a real engineering firm and watching it work, is what Echology builds on.

I started Echology because I watched the same pattern repeat everywhere. Teams get access to LLMs, embeddings, vector databases, and then throw raw documents at the model because nobody showed them there's a step before that. A structural step where you classify what matters before reasoning begins.

Decompose is the deterministic foundation. AECai is the first vertical module, built for AEC firms. Geode applies the same platform to AI visibility optimization. Each module is a configuration of the same architecture, not a separate product.
